Kenya

Kenya is an underdeveloped country in Africa and many people do not think of visiting it however, if you are someone who is looking for a charity trip, education on different cultures and more look no further. MEtoWe is a social enterprise that holds charity trips to educate people on their five main pillars and teach you how to apply them in helping underdevelopied countries. So they are action based instead and allow you to experience the reality of the struggling countries in order to see from their perspective and take action.



The Five pillars

Me to We focus on five different pillars that they believe are the most important to make a change. Stating them will also highlight how connected they are. The five pillars include health, food, water, opportunity and education. Health is a vital aspect because if the child or parent is not healthy they will not be able to attend school, food security is another pillar as hungry or malnurished children will obviously be inattentive and sick and will not be able to attend school. The opportunity pillar focuses on the fact that children will only be able to attend school if their parents have financal support and time to invest in their education. The next pillar is water and children (especially girls) can only attend school if they have access to clean water because having reliable water for their families will free theme from their daily task of collecting water and will prevent waterborn illnesses. The final pillar is education, empowering people, especially children with the knowledge, skills and confidence to develop their capabilaties is the best way to set them up for success and break the cycle of poverty. Where does Me to We come in all this? Well, they partner with communities to implement sustainable clean water as well as hand pumps, they implement health initatives such as hospitals, moobile clinics and vaccination programs,they place programs that promote food security and improved agriculture and irrigation projects to establashing local farms, they work with families to implement productive resoureses such as goats and provide relevant financial literacy training to generate incomes and finally they ensure children have access to quality education by bulding schools.

Safari

Kenya is one of the world's magnificent wildlife destinations. It is home to some of the highest and most diverse populations of wildlife on the planet. It offeres a unique chance to see the wildlife in flesh! An african safari is the only way you can track down wild animals and watch their beauty and power. In my visit there I was able to see a lion close up, giraffes and gizzells running around and got to picnic over the view of the hippos. This attraction is a very recommended once in a lifetime experience.
